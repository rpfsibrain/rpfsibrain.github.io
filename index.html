Research website · computational mechanics and materials

Mechanics of soft, smart, and biomedical systems.

My research combines finite-element modeling, fluid-structure interaction, nonlinear material modeling, experiments, and design optimization to study deployable endovascular devices, magnetic soft robotic swimmers and swarms, multifunctional soft materials, hydrogels, xerogels, and piezoceramic/piezocomposite systems.

Research areas

From biomedical devices to soft material mechanics.

The research areas below are grouped by scientific theme rather than chronology. Together they show a continuous line from material response and smart composites to soft robotic locomotion and biomedical device simulation.

Endovascular devices

Computational placement of braided implants

High-fidelity FEM of braided endovascular devices for intracranial aneurysm treatment, including crimping, catheter release, wall contact, friction, device expansion, and future patient-specific FSI.

  • braided beam geometries
  • contact mechanics and deployment
  • aneurysm-oriented biomedical design

Representative source: IDIR Kiel poster, 2025.

Magnetic soft robots

Low-Reynolds-number swimmer design

Computational and experimental design of miniaturized magnetic soft robotic swimmers, linking magnetization, elasticity, geometry, drag, stability, and swimming speed.

  • helical and undulatory propulsion
  • experiment-model comparison
  • design maps for fast versatile swimmers

Highlighted paper: Extreme Mechanics Letters, 2025.

Collective dynamics

Soft robotic swarms and emergent patterns

Study of multi-swimmer interaction, nonreciprocal hydrodynamics, attraction-repulsion mechanisms, radial spreading, and three-dimensional collective trajectories.

  • two- and three-swimmer systems
  • spatiotemporal pattern formation
  • soft active matter and microrobotics

Highlighted paper: Nonlinear Dynamics, 2025.

Smart soft materials

Hard-magnetic soft material modeling

Thermodynamically consistent micromechanical and constitutive models for hard-magnetic soft materials, accounting for magneto-mechanics, hyperelasticity, plasticity, and viscous effects.

  • dipole-dipole interactions
  • polymer network mechanics
  • magneto-mechanical coupling

Representative paper: Mechanics of Materials, 2023.

Biomedical soft materials

Hydrogels, xerogels, and bio-composites

Experimental and theoretical characterization of soft polymeric materials, PVA hydrogels/xerogels, silk-cellulose composites, nanocellulose reinforcement, and biomedical material response.

  • morphology-mechanics relation
  • viscoelastic and rate-dependent behavior
  • biomaterial architecture

Representative papers: JMBBM, Materials Letters, IJES, MTDM.

Piezoceramics

Piezocomposites and electromechanical creep

Pre-PhD work on 1-3 piezocomposites, piezoceramics, ferroelectric/dielectric response, creep-fatigue degradation, thermo-electro-mechanical modeling, and smart material characterization.

  • creep and fatigue experiments
  • phenomenological constitutive modeling
  • piezoelectric and ferroelectric materials

Representative papers: Acta Mechanica, Ceramics International, Meccanica, Philosophical Magazine.

Endovascular mechanics

Deployable braided implants as nonlinear mechanical systems

The current postdoctoral project treats the Contour device as a deployable braided mechanical structure whose final configuration depends on geometry, contact, catheter release, friction, and aneurysm morphology.

Computational crimping and release of a braided device.
Contact-driven device-wall interaction during in silico placement.
Path towards patient-specific deployment and flow-diversion analysis.
Poster
Soft robotic locomotion

Fast and versatile magnetic swimmers at low Reynolds number

Experiments and simulations identify how geometry and magnetic actuation generate efficient swimming, stability, and bi-directionality in helical and undulatory magnetic soft robotic swimmers.

Finger-shaped swimmers show high forward performance.
Carangiform-like swimmers provide robust undulatory motion.
Performance maps translate parameters into design rules.
DOIarXiv
Collective behavior

Hydrodynamic interaction produces organized soft robotic collectives

Soft robotic swarms reveal attraction, repulsion, oscillatory lateral drift, and collective trajectory patterns without prescribing explicit flocking rules.

Axially separated swimmers tend to approach one another.
Laterally separated swimmers drift outward to reduce interaction.
Three-swimmer systems reveal emergent three-dimensional structure.
DOIarXiv
Soft materials before and beyond the PhD

Hydrogels, xerogels, silk-cellulose composites, and fractal rheology

The soft-material work links microstructure, reinforcement, porosity, viscoelasticity, and nonlinear material response, with applications in biomedical materials and soft multifunctional systems.

Nanocellulose loading changes PVA xerogel morphology, strength, brittleness, creep, and relaxation.
Fractal viscoelastic modeling provides a compact framework for broad classes of soft polymeric materials.
Hydrogels and silk/cellulose composites connect morphology with mechanical function.
